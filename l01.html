Crime Mapping and Spatial Analysis

Lab 1: Mapping Human Spatial Behavior

John Palmer

Task 1

    Observe the activity going on downstairs in the atrium. Using a pen and paper, draw a map or schematic diagram conveying the information you find most interesting about the way people are using this space.

Task 2

    Open QGIS and follow the First Steps with QGIS recipe to create a basic map of this neighborhood using the Open Street Map tiles, and manually place a marker indicating our location.

Task 3

    Follow the Mapping Basics recipe to make a map of reported assaults in New York City in 2018. Add a scale bar and legend and generate a PNG file of your map.

Lab Report (turn in via Aula Global before Thursday lecture)

  • A digial photograph of your map from Task 1
  • The PNG file of your map from Task 3
  • Short answers to the following questions:

    • What strikes you about the spatial pattern of the crimes you have mapped?
    • What biases do you think there are in taking this information as a proxy for criminal activity in New York City?
    • What types of crimes are not reported in the data set?
